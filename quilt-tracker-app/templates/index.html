How It Works

Your quilt kit is organized in layers. Cut pieces are sewn into blocks — directly when a block is simple, or first grouped into segments when a block has too many pieces to combine in one pass. Blocks become your quilt.

This app tracks your progress at every level, and gives you two ways to work through the cutting.

Quilt

The finished project. All blocks arranged in a labeled grid — rows A–H, columns 1–8. The grid is your map.

Each cell in the grid is a block (e.g. A4). Simple blocks are sewn directly from pieces. More complex blocks group their pieces into segments first.

Segments

A segment (e.g. A4a, A4b) is an optional intermediate sewing unit used when a block has too many pieces to combine in one pass. The assembly guide shows the order to join segments.

Individual fabric cuts. Each piece has two numbers on the cut guide: a number in a circle (identifies the shape in the cut graphic) and a number in parentheses (its sew sequence within a segment or block). Pieces are sewn into segments — or directly into a block when there are no segments.

Two ways to work

Quilt View

Work block by block. Click a block on the grid, check off pieces and segments as you cut them, then step through the assembly guide.

Best when you work one block at a time from start to finish.

Colors View

Work color by color. Click a fabric tile to see every piece of that color across all blocks, and check them off in one run.

Best when you cut an entire fabric before moving to the next color.

Block Detail

Click a block on the quilt to see its pieces and track progress.
